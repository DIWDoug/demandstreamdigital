# SEO Competition Framework (LC/MC/HC)

## Overview
Local SEO pricing and scope are determined by **market competition and execution intensity**, not business size, emotion, or guesswork. Different markets require different levels of sustained effort to rank, grow, and hold position.

## Low Competition (LC)
**Stable, lightly contested, forgiving markets**

### Typical Industries
- Landscaping, lawn care, cleaning services
- Junk removal, pressure washing, window cleaning
- Pool service, appliance repair
- Florists, photographers, tutors
- Small single-location service businesses in small/mid-sized towns

### Characteristics
- Low SERP saturation
- Few authoritative competitors
- Slower ranking volatility
- Modest review velocity requirements
- Minimal backlink competition

### Requirements
- Strong technical and on-page SEO foundations
- GBP optimization and maintenance
- Citation consistency and monitoring
- Moderate local link acquisition
- Steady but controlled content growth

## Medium Competition (MC)
**Active, competitive, winnable with consistency**

### Typical Industries
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical in mid-sized metros
- Roofing, garage doors, pest control
- Auto repair and towing
- Gyms, veterinarians, restaurants
- Accounting, insurance, chiropractic in non-major metros

### Characteristics
- Competitors investing inconsistently
- Some authoritative players, but no total dominance
- Rankings move with sustained effort
- Reviews, links, and content matter, but not at extreme levels

### Requirements
- Ongoing on-page and GBP optimization
- Consistent citation and review management
- Moderate, quality-controlled link velocity
- Local content and service-area expansion
- Regular competitive monitoring and tuning

## High Competition (HC)
**Saturated, expensive, unforgiving markets**

### Typical Industries
- Legal, medical, dental
- Med spas, plastic surgery, laser hair removal
- Real estate, mortgage, insurance
- Highly competitive home services in major metros
- Multi-location and franchise businesses

### Characteristics
- Saturated Local Pack and organic SERPs
- High CPCs indicating strong demand
- Aggressive review and authority competition
- Volatile rankings
- Continuous competitive pressure

### Requirements
- Sustained link velocity and authority building
- Aggressive, compliant review acquisition and management
- Continuous content creation and optimization
- Advanced citation cleanup and suppression
- Ongoing competitive analysis and strategy adjustments

## Small Town Considerations

Population alone does not determine competition level. **Market reality does.**

### May drop one tier if:
- Very few real competitors
- Competitors have weak reviews, links, and content
- CPCs and paid competition are low
- Growth goals are steady, not aggressive

### Remains HC if:
- Within commuting distance of a major metro
- One or two dominant competitors control visibility
- Service is high-ticket and comparison-driven
- Aggressive growth is the goal

**Note:** It is rare for medical or aesthetic services to qualify as LC, even in small towns.

## Non-Negotiable Guardrails

1. Competition level is based on **market conditions**, not business size
2. Aggressive growth goals push clients up a tier
3. Major metros increase competition pressure
4. Pricing and delivery must align to tier or quality suffers
5. Scope outside a tier is additional and not absorbed
